Embracing Diversity: Disability and Inclusion – Part 2

Family Acceptance: The Foundation of Empowerment

Disability could start from one's home and end there as well. When the family members of a disabled person decide to treat them as an equal, it not only boosts their morale but also helps them realize their potential and motivates them to pursue their goals. As Sigmund Freud once said, "How bold one gets, when one is sure of being loved."

Societal Perception and Its Impact

When a disabled person is respected within their family, they begin to desire the same level of respect from society—especially from those who might otherwise view them indifferently. Once a person recognizes what they truly deserve, they become determined to achieve it. Unfortunately, society often deters disabled people from progressing by constantly reminding them of their limitations, thereby making them believe they are less capable or even futile.

Redefining Ability: Beyond Overcoming Disability

We don't need to overcome disability; instead, we can focus on changing our definition of ability. When families and communities empower disabled individuals by recognizing their worth, it opens the door for these individuals to excel without excuses. By celebrating unique strengths and fostering inclusive environments, we pave the way for a future where every person is valued for their true potential.
